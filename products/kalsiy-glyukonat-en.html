Calcium Gluconate 10% Injection Solution

5 ml or 10 ml ampoules, in a pack of 10

Description

Calcium gluconate is an injectable solution that supports bone tissue and cardiac muscle function, with antiallergic and anti-inflammatory effects. It is used in cases of calcium deficiency in the body.

Indications

  • Calcium deficiency (hypocalcemia)
  • Allergic diseases (as adjunctive therapy)
  • Inflammatory conditions
  • Reduced cardiac muscle tone
  • Osteoporosis and bone fractures

Dosage and Administration

The solution is administered intravenously or intramuscularly. The dosage is determined by a physician, typically 5–10 ml (10%) once a day for adults.

Contraindications

  • Hypercalcemia
  • Severe renal failure
  • Increased blood clotting (risk of thrombosis)
  • Hypersensitivity to the components of the drug

Storage Conditions

Store in a dry, light-protected place at temperatures from 5°C to 25°C. Keep out of reach of children. Shelf life: 3 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can calcium gluconate be administered intramuscularly?

Yes, but with caution. It is recommended to warm the solution and inject slowly.

When is intravenous administration preferred?

Intravenous administration is preferred in emergencies or when high doses are required.

Are there any drug interactions?

Some antibiotics should not be used simultaneously. Always consult a doctor.
